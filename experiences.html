Island Experiences

From cliff dives to coconut sweets everything you can see, taste, and feel in Siquijor.

Get Active

Adventure & Outdoor Activities

Whether you're here to jump off cliffs, dive into coral gardens, or circle the island on a scooter, Siquijor's natural playground has no shortage of thrills.

Cliff Diving at Salagdoong

Take the leap from platforms 6 to 32 feet above the crystal-clear water. Salagdoong Beach Resort has maintained this thrilling spot for years, with a waterslide alternative for the less brave.

Adrenaline Swimming ₱30 entry

Snorkeling & Scuba Diving

The waters around Siquijor are rich with coral reefs, colorful fish, anemones, and occasionally sea turtles. Rent snorkel gear or join an organized dive tour to the best spots.

Marine Life Coral Reefs All levels

Island Circumnavigation by Motorbike

Follow the 76 km coastal road all the way around the island. Dramatic ocean views, hidden coves, lush mountain roads, and colorful local life all in one epic day ride.

Self-guided Scenic ₱300–₱500/day

Rope Swings at Cambugahay Falls

Tarzan-swing yourself into the electric-turquoise pools of Cambugahay Falls. One of the most iconic and photogenic experiences on the island. Bamboo raft rides available too.

Swimming Instagram-worthy ₱30 entry

Cave Spelunking at Cantabon

Don a life vest and wade, crawl, and swim through Cantabon Cave's ancient passages. Stalactites, underground rivers, and glittering formations await. A proper adventure!

Adventure Guide required All ages

Hiking Mt. Bandilaan

Trek through the island's national park forest to the highest peak on Siquijor (557m). Sweeping views of the entire island and surrounding Visayan Sea reward the effort.

Nature Hiking National Park
Culture & Tradition

Mystical Siquijor

Beyond the natural beauty lies a culture unlike anywhere else in the Philippines one woven with folklore, healing traditions, and centuries of Spanish heritage.

Folk Healing Traditions

Siquijor is famous across the Philippines for its folk healers (mananambal) and traditional medicine practitioners, who use local herbs and rituals passed down through generations.

Spanish Colonial Heritage

The island's churches especially Lazi and San Isidro de Labrador are magnificent examples of Baroque architecture, some over 400 years old and still standing.

Healing Festival (Lenten Season)

Every Holy Week, folk healers from across the island gather in San Antonio to collect herbs, prepare potions, and share ancient healing rituals in a uniquely Siquijodnon tradition.

Island of Fire (Isla del Fuego)

Named by Spanish explorers who saw forests glowing with fireflies at night. This mystical reputation adds a magical layer to every sunset walk and evening stroll.

Food & Dining

Flavors of the Island

From traditional Filipino delicacies to surprisingly excellent international cuisine Siquijor's food scene is a delightful discovery.

Torta

Sweet traditional Filipino cake Siquijor's most beloved local delicacy.

Kinilaw

Filipino ceviche with ultra fresh fish, vinegar, ginger & chili.

Bukayo

Chewy coconut candy made with young coconut and sugar.

Pan Bisaya

Traditional Visayan breakfast bread, best enjoyed with coffee.

Chupa-Chupa

Sweet homemade caramel candy a nostalgic island treat.

Kalamay

Sticky sweet glutinous rice dessert wrapped in coconut leaves.

Fruit Wine

Homemade wines from mango, pineapple, and local fruits. A unique island souvenir.

Salwaki

Sea urchin a prized local seafood delicacy, best eaten fresh from the shore.

Where to Stay

From beachfront resorts to jungle treehouses, Siquijor has accommodation to match every style and budget.

Coco Grove Beach Resort

The island's top resort with a 1 km private beach, two pools, spa, dive center, and outstanding service. Perfect for those wanting a luxurious, all-in-one island getaway.

San Juan · ₱₱₱₱ · Best resort on island

Nakabalo Guesthouse

Perched on the hills with a jaw-dropping jungle-meets-ocean view and an infinity pool that will make you question reality. Day passes available for non-guests.
