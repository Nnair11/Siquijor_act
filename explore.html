Explore Siquijor

Discover the island's most breathtaking spots from glowing waterfalls to mystical ancient trees.

The Island of Fire

A World of Wonders Awaits

Small in size but enormous in natural splendor, Siquijor is home to over 20 major tourist attractions all within reach of a single day's motorbike ride around the circumferential road.

All Spots Beaches Waterfalls Nature Heritage
Waterfall

Cambugahay Falls

Three-tiered cascades of electric turquoise water fed by natural springs. Swim, swing on ropes, and float on bamboo rafts in one of the Philippines' most stunning falls.

Lazi ₱30 entry All day
Beach

Paliton Beach

The island's most iconic beach powdery white sand, towering coconut palms, and world-class sunsets over the Mindanao Sea. Perfect for swimming and snorkeling.

San Juan Best at sunset
Beach + Diving

Salagdoong Beach

A stunning beach most famous for its concrete cliff-diving platforms one at 6 feet and one at 32 feet. There's even a waterslide for those who prefer a gentler plunge.

Maria ₱30 entry Thrilling
Nature

Old Enchanted Balete Tree

A 400-year-old balete tree, one of the largest in Asia, surrounded by a natural pool where tiny fish nibble your feet for a natural foot spa. Eerily beautiful.

San Antonio Mystical
Heritage

Lazi Church & Convent

A UNESCO-nominated Baroque church built in 1884, paired with one of the oldest and largest convents in Asia. A stunning piece of colonial Spanish architecture.

Lazi Heritage site Free
Cave

Cantabon Cave

A thrilling spelunking adventure through a cave system with stalactites, stalagmites, and an underground river you actually swim through. Guides are required and professional.

Cantabon Guide required Swim inside
Nature

Mt. Bandilaan

The island's highest peak at 557m above sea level, offering sweeping panoramic views of the island and surrounding seas. A national park with lush forest trails.

Central Siquijor National Park
Nature

Siquijor Butterfly Sanctuary

A peaceful family-friendly sanctuary home to dozens of endemic butterfly species fluttering among tropical flowers. Charming and calming in equal measure.

San Juan Family-friendly
Waterfall

Lugnason Falls (Zodiac Falls)

A hidden waterfall off the main tourist trail part of a series of 12 falls representing the zodiac signs. Jungle trekking, tranquility, and natural pools without the crowds.

San Juan Less crowded
Getting Around

Explore at Your Own Pace

The best way to see Siquijor is to rent a motorbike (₱300–₱500/day) and follow the 76 km circumferential road around the island. You can complete the full loop in about 2–3 hours, or take your time stopping at every spot you fancy.

  • Motorbike / Scooter
    Most popular option. ₱300–₱500/day. Ask your accommodation to arrange one.
  • Tricycle Tours
    Hire a tricycle and driver for a guided half-day or full-day island tour. ₱800–₱1,500.
  • Guided Tours
    Tour operators offer all-inclusive island tours with a knowledgeable local guide.
